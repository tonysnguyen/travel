Getting There & Back

The Journey

Every leg, both ways. Click a tab to see the outbound route to Koh Phangan or the return trip home to Seattle.

1
✈ International Flight
~11 hrs
Seattle → Taipei
Sat, Feb 6, 2027 · SEA → TPE (Taoyuan) · EVA Air / China Airlines · ~$800–1,400 USD roundtrip
The big one — crossing the Pacific. Book a window seat on the right side of the plane for views of the North Pacific. Bring a neck pillow, noise-cancelling headphones, and melatonin. The flight departs SEA typically in the evening, arriving in Taipei the next morning local time.
EVA Air's Royal Laurel business class is one of Asia's best if you're treating yourself — also search for premium economy deals which run ~$1,800 roundtrip.
2
✈ Connecting Flight
~3.5 hrs
Taipei → Bangkok
Sun, Feb 7, 2027 · TPE → BKK (Suvarnabhumi) · EVA Air / Thai Airways · Included in through-ticket
A comfortable mid-length hop. Taipei Taoyuan Airport (TPE) has excellent food, lounge access, and transit hotels if you have a long layover. Stock up on Taiwanese snacks for the road — the pineapple cakes from the airport shops are excellent.
Allow at least 2.5 hours for your connection in TPE. Immigration lines can be long at peak times.
3
✈ Domestic Flight
~1.5 hrs
Bangkok → Koh Samui
Mon, Feb 8, 2027 · BKK → USM · Bangkok Airways · ~$80–150 USD one-way
Bangkok Airways is a boutique airline with a surprisingly premium experience including a complimentary lounge at BKK. Samui Airport (USM) is one of the world's most beautiful airports — an open-air terminal surrounded by palm trees. No jetway — you walk across the tarmac.
Book Bangkok Airways early — this route sells out fast during high season (Dec–Feb) and Full Moon Party weeks.
4
⛴ High-Speed Ferry
~30 min
Koh Samui → Koh Phangan
Mon, Feb 8, 2027 · Nathon Pier → Thong Sala Pier · Lomprayah or Seatran Ferry · ~300–400 THB
Multiple ferries run daily with the first departing around 7 AM. The crossing takes about 30 minutes on a high-speed catamaran. Book ahead during Full Moon Party week — the ferry fills up fast both ways. Sit on the upper deck for the arrival view of Koh Phangan's green mountains.
Lomprayah is slightly faster and more comfortable. Seatran is cheaper. Both are reliable.
5
🏍️ Local Transport
~35 min
Thong Sala Pier → Chaloklum
Mon, Feb 8, 2027 · Evening · ~30–40 min · Songthaew shared taxi or private taxi · ~150–400 THB
Songthaew trucks and private taxis wait right at the pier. Always agree on the price before getting in. The scenic ride to Chaloklum village winds through mountains and coconut groves. A private taxi to Chaloklum runs about 400 THB ($12 USD) — cheaper if you share with other passengers.
Grab a local SIM card at 7-Eleven near the pier before your taxi — you'll need maps to navigate the island.
1
🏍️ Local Transport
~35 min
Chaloklum → Thong Sala Pier
Sat, Feb 20, 2027 · Morning · ~30–40 min · Songthaew or private taxi · ~150–400 THB
Grab a private taxi or songthaew from Chaloklum village to Thong Sala Pier on the south of the island. The ride winds back through the same mountain and coconut grove scenery you arrived through. Budget extra time during Full Moon Party week — traffic can back up near the pier.
Book your onward ferry ticket online the night before to avoid pier queues. Lomprayah and Seatran both offer online booking.
2
⛴ High-Speed Ferry
~30 min
Koh Phangan → Koh Samui
Sat, Feb 20, 2027 · Thong Sala Pier → Nathon/Bangrak Pier · Lomprayah or Seatran · ~300–400 THB
The short island hop back to Koh Samui. Multiple departures throughout the day — the earliest is around 7 AM, last ferry around 6 PM. Take one last look back at Koh Phangan's green mountains as you leave. Upper deck gives the best view.
Confirm which pier on Koh Samui the ferry arrives at — Bangrak and Nathon are both used, and each is a different distance from the airport.
3
✈ Domestic Flight
~1.5 hrs
Koh Samui → Bangkok
Sat, Feb 20, 2027 · USM → BKK (Suvarnabhumi) · Bangkok Airways · ~$80–150 USD one-way
The return leg through Samui Airport (USM) — still one of the world's most scenic airports. Bangkok Airways provides a complimentary lounge here too, so arrive early to enjoy it. The flight brings you back into the cool, air-conditioned world of Suvarnabhumi Airport.
Allow at least 2.5–3 hours between your Samui arrival and your Bangkok international departure — ferry delays can happen.
4
✈ Connecting Flight
~3.5 hrs
Bangkok → Taipei
Sat, Feb 20, 2027 · BKK → TPE (Taoyuan) · EVA Air / Thai Airways · Included in through-ticket
The hop back to Taipei Taoyuan for your Pacific crossing connection. A comfortable flight — use the time to decompress, write down your training highlights, or stock up on Taiwanese souvenirs in the terminal if you have a layover. TPE has some of Asia's best airport food.
EVA Air's transit lounge at TPE is excellent if you have access. Great for a shower and meal before the long haul home.
5
✈ Trans-Pacific
~11 hrs
Taipei → Seattle
Sun, Feb 21, 2027 · TPE → SEA · EVA Air / China Airlines · Included in roundtrip fare
The long Pacific crossing home. You'll likely arrive at SEA in the morning after an overnight flight. Customs and immigration at SEA are well-managed — have your declaration card ready. You'll step off the plane fitter, sharper, and carrying a whole new level of Muay Thai.
Set your watch to Seattle time as soon as you board. Use the first few hours of the flight to stay awake and align with home time — it helps minimize jet lag on arrival.
Before You Leave Seattle

Mission Prep Checklist

35 items across 5 categories. Check them off as you pack and prepare for the journey.

Mission Readiness 0 / 35
🎉 All packed! You're ready for Koh Phangan. Train hard. 🥊
  • ✓
    Passport — Valid 6+ Months
    Thailand grants US citizens 30-day visa-free entry. Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your travel date. Check the expiry date NOW.
  • ✓
    Travel Insurance with Sports Coverage
    Standard travel insurance won't cover Muay Thai training injuries. You MUST select a policy that explicitly covers adventure sports or martial arts. Recommended: World Nomads, SafetyWing, Battleface.
  • ✓
    Digital & Physical Document Copies
    Store digital copies of passport, insurance, and booking confirmations in email and cloud storage. Keep physical copies in a separate bag from originals.
  • ✓
    Thai Baht — Know the Rate
    1 USD ≈ 33–35 THB. Familiarise yourself with rough conversions before arriving so you're not overcharged at markets.
  • ✓
    Exchange at Superrich Bangkok
    Best rates in Bangkok — use Superrich Thailand, found in major malls and near Suvarnabhumi Airport. Avoid airport currency kiosks — terrible rates.
  • ✓
    Wise or Schwab Debit Card
    ATMs on Koh Phangan charge ~200 THB fee per withdrawal. Wise and Charles Schwab debit cards have no foreign transaction fees and reimburse ATM fees globally.
  • ✓
    Cash for Local Markets & Taxis
    Credit cards accepted at resorts and gyms, but local markets, songthaew taxis, and street food stalls are cash only. Keep 2,000–5,000 THB on hand at all times.
  • ✓
    Hand Wraps (2+ pairs)
    They get completely soaked after each session. Bring at least 2 pairs so one dries while you train with the other. 180" cotton or Mexican-style wraps.
  • ✓
    Boxing Gloves (8–16 oz)
    Weight depends on your body weight and use. Typically 8–10 oz for bag and pad work, 12–16 oz for sparring. Bring your own for hygiene and fit.
  • ✓
    Shin Guards
    Essential for sparring and partner drills. Leather guards last longer in tropical humidity than foam. Make sure they cover the top of the foot.
  • ✓
    Mouth Guard
    Non-negotiable for any contact or sparring work. A custom-fit dental guard offers the best protection and comfort.
  • ✓
    Groin Protector
    Mandatory for sparring — you will take kicks. Do not show up to a Thai gym without this.
  • ✓
    Ankle Supports
    Muay Thai pivots and roundhouse kicks stress the ankles significantly. Elastic ankle wraps or sleeve-style supports help prevent common sprains.
  • ✓
    Muay Thai Shorts
    Wide-cut for high kicks and leg movement. The gym may sell them on-site. Markets on the island carry great Thai-made options at excellent prices.
  • ✓
    Rash Guard / Compression Shorts
    Great for clinch work to reduce skin friction. Quick-dry synthetic material only — no cotton.
  • ✓
    Extra Training Shirts (3–4 minimum)
    You will soak through a shirt every single session. Pack more than you think you need. Polyester or nylon quick-dry only.
  • ✓
    Sports Sandals / Flip Flops
    For walking around the gym between rounds and in shared shower areas. Essential for gym hygiene.
  • ✓
    Electrolytes — ESSENTIAL
    You WILL sweat heavily training twice daily in tropical heat. Bring Nuun tablets, LMNT packets, or Liquid IV sachets. Stock up before leaving — expensive and hard to find in Thailand.
  • ✓
    Ibuprofen / Naproxen
    For muscle soreness and inflammation from heavy training. Available in Thailand but bring your preferred brand.
  • ✓
    Tiger Balm
    Widely available throughout Thailand at great prices. Buy it on the island — great for muscle recovery and bruising.
  • ✓
    Imodium / Anti-Diarrheal
    Very common for first-timers in Southeast Asia adjusting to new food and water environments.
  • ✓
    Probiotics
    Start 1–2 weeks before departure. Helps your gut adapt to new food and water — significantly reduces stomach issues.
  • ✓
    Antihistamines
    For tropical bug bites and allergies. Cetirizine (Zyrtec) or loratadine are non-drowsy options.
  • ✓
    Sunscreen SPF 50+
    Training outdoors under tropical sun is brutal. Apply before every outdoor session and reapply every 2 hours. Use reef-safe formula.
  • ✓
    DEET Insect Repellent
    Mosquitoes are real near Chaloklum's jungle and mountain areas. Use 30–50% DEET in the evenings.
  • ✓
    First Aid Kit
    Blister pads, kinesiology tape, antiseptic wipes, bandages. Training blisters are inevitable — be prepared.
  • ✓
    Prescription Medications
    Bring a full supply plus 1–2 weeks extra. Keep in carry-on luggage — never in checked bags.
  • ✓
    Quick-Dry Workout Clothes (3–4 sets)
    Training twice daily means laundry piles up fast. Polyester and nylon only — cotton is miserable in tropical heat.
  • ✓
    Lightweight Casual Clothes
    Light linen or cotton for evenings and rest days. Keep it minimal and breathable.
  • ✓
    Rain Jacket
    Thailand gets sudden heavy downpours with no warning. A packable rain jacket weighs almost nothing.
  • ✓
    Reef-Safe Sunscreen
    For swimming and snorkeling. Standard sunscreens harm coral reefs — use mineral-based, reef-safe formulas.
  • ✓
    Water Bottle / Hydration Pack
    Drink constantly — especially during outdoor training. An insulated 1L+ bottle keeps water cooler in the heat.
  • ✓
    Power Adapter
    Thailand uses Type A/B/C outlets — US Type A plugs often work directly. Bring a universal adapter to cover all bases.
  • ✓
    Portable Power Bank
    The Seattle → Koh Phangan journey is 24+ hours door to door. A 10,000–20,000 mAh bank is ideal.
  • ✓
    Earplugs
    Koh Phangan is the Full Moon Party island — monthly all-night beach raves audible island-wide. Non-negotiable on those nights.
